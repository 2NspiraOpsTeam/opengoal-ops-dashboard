# Open Goal Soccer — Email Follow-up SOP + SLA

Last Updated: 2026-03-03  
Owners: Brian (Qualification Ops), Maya (Comms), Adam (Strategy/Optimization)

## 1) Purpose
Establish a single team standard for monitoring and handling outreach inbox follow-ups so responses are fast, qualified, and consistently routed.

## 2) Scope
Applies to all inbound replies from vetted Open Goal Soccer outreach campaigns.

## 3) Team Roles and Ownership
- **Maya (Comms Owner)**
  - Monitors inbox 3x per business day
  - Sends first-touch responses and nurture follow-ups
  - Maintains message templates by segment
- **Brian (Qualification Owner)**
  - Reviews positive/neutral replies for fit
  - Scores fit: High / Medium / Low
  - Routes high-fit leads to priority queue
- **Adam (Strategy/Intel Owner)**
  - Reviews weekly metrics and response patterns
  - Recommends testing plan (copy/segment/CTA)
  - Publishes weekly optimization memo

## 4) Daily Monitoring Cadence
Business days (PT):
- Morning check: 8:30–9:30 AM
- Midday check: 12:00–1:00 PM
- Late check: 4:00–5:00 PM

If high-volume day, add an extra sweep at ~2:30 PM.

## 5) Triage Workflow (Operational)
1. **Capture** inbound reply and timestamp
2. **Classify intent**
   - Interested
   - Needs info
   - Not now
   - Not a fit / unsubscribe
3. **Brian fit score**
   - High / Medium / Low
4. **Route**
   - High: priority response path
   - Medium: standard response + follow-up
   - Low: polite close or low-frequency nurture
5. **Log outcome** in outreach tracker
6. **Schedule next action** (if any)

## 6) Service Level Agreement (SLA)
- **Inbox triage target:** within **4 business hours**
- **High-fit lead response target:** within **2 business hours**
- **Standard response target:** same business day
- **Escalation target:** blockers resolved within 1 business day

## 7) Follow-up Cadence
For non-closed threads:
- Day 2 follow-up
- Day 5 follow-up
- Day 10 final follow-up

After Day 10:
- Move to nurture OR close as no response.

## 8) Message Quality Standards
- Keep responses clear, short, and role-appropriate
- Include one clear CTA per message
- Use segment-specific templates (do not use one-size-fits-all copy)
- Personalize with school/program context when available

## 9) KPI Dashboard (Weekly)
Track at minimum:
- Deliverability: bounce rate, spam/complaint flags
- Open rate
- Reply rate
- Positive-interest rate
- Qualified-opportunity rate
- Time to first response

## 10) Weekly Optimization Loop
Every week (30–45 min):
1. Review KPI movement and bottlenecks
2. Identify top 1–2 hypotheses
3. Launch controlled test(s): subject line, CTA, segment framing
4. Keep winners; sunset underperformers
5. Update templates and SOP notes

## 11) Escalation Rules
Escalate to team channel immediately if:
- Complaint/spam pattern increases
- Bounce spike > baseline
- Any legal/compliance concern appears
- High-fit lead requests meeting and no owner assigned

## 12) Change Control
All process updates are PR-based in this repository.
- Brian owns operations updates
- Maya owns comms-template updates
- Adam owns KPI/testing framework updates
