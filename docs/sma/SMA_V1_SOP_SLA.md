# Social Media Advantage (SMA) — SOP + SLA (v1)

## Purpose
Create consistent, high-value, human-centered engagement across Jeffrey’s Facebook groups to increase awareness and trust for:
- Open Goal Soccer
- 2Nspira
- Jeffrey’s professional brand (book, tech, leadership)

## Scope
Applies to daily Facebook group monitoring, candidate reply generation, approvals, posting workflow, and follow-up tracking.

## Team Operating Lenses
- **Maya (Comms Lead):** Final voice, tone adaptation, posting, and relationship continuity.
- **Adam (Discovery Lead):** Post discovery, context enrichment, trend scanning, sourcing references.
- **Brian (Ops/Risk Lead):** Qualification criteria, controls, SLA governance, KPI scorecard, escalation.
- **Jeffrey (Principal):** Final authority for strategy pivots and sensitive escalations.

---

## 1) SOP Workflow

### A. Daily Run Cadence (Cron)
- Run 2–4 times daily (recommended: 9:00a, 12:30p, 4:00p, 8:00p ET).
- Each run produces:
  1. Candidate post queue
  2. Suggested response drafts
  3. Priority/risk tags

### B. Post Qualification (Adam → Brian rules)
A post enters queue when at least one is true:
- Direct relevance to youth sports, coaching, parent support, school/community athletics (OGS lane)
- Direct relevance to leadership, automation, operations, tech enablement (2Nspira lane)
- Clear ask where Jeffrey’s lived/professional perspective helps

Exclude by default:
- Political or polarizing threads unrelated to mission
- Medical/legal advice requests beyond competence
- Threads where affiliate insertion would feel opportunistic

### C. Response Construction (Maya)
Each approved response should include:
1. Empathy/recognition of poster’s context
2. 1–2 practical value points (actionable)
3. Optional resource mention (OGS/2Nspira/book)
4. Optional affiliate recommendation only if problem-solution fit is explicit
5. Soft CTA (invite dialogue; no hard-sell)

### D. Posting Modes
- **Auto-approve lane (Low Risk):** informational/supportive comments without external links
- **Review-required lane (Med Risk):** branded links or nuanced claims
- **Manual-only lane (High Risk):** controversy, admin-sensitive groups, legal/health claims, reputation-sensitive topics

### E. Follow-up Handling
- Monitor replies to posted comments in next 24h window.
- For engagement opportunities:
  - Route OGS inquiries to outreach/funnel tracker
  - Route 2Nspira prospects to business development tracker
  - Route book/personal brand asks to content follow-up list

---

## 2) SLA Targets

## A. Discovery-to-Draft SLA
- Candidate post identified to first draft: **≤ 60 minutes** during active windows

## B. Draft-to-Post SLA
- Low risk: **≤ 2 hours**
- Medium risk: **≤ 4 business hours**
- High risk: **manual review required; same day target**

## C. Follow-up SLA
- First response to direct question/reply on our comment: **≤ 6 business hours**
- Priority opportunities: **≤ 2 business hours**

## D. Data Logging SLA
- All posted engagements logged same day with status and outcome tag

---

## 3) Response Quality Standard (Definition of Done)
A response is “done” when all are true:
- Helpful and context-specific
- Human tone, not template-spam
- No overclaiming
- Aligns with OGS/2Nspira values
- Correct disclosure present if affiliate link used
- Logged to tracker with post URL and outcome status

---

## 4) Escalation Rules
Escalate to Jeffrey when:
- Media/PR sensitivity is possible
- Conflict with group admin/moderator appears
- Potential legal/reputational concerns
- Strategic partnership opportunity emerges

Escalation bundle must include:
- Thread URL
- Situation summary
- Risk level
- Recommended response options
