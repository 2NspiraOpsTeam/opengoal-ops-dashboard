# Social Media Advantage (SMA) — Risk Policy (v1)

## Policy Objective
Protect Jeffrey’s reputation, account integrity, and brand trust while scaling useful engagement in Facebook groups.

## Core Principles
1. **Human-centered before growth-centered**
2. **Help first, sell second**
3. **Truthful, transparent, respectful**
4. **Platform-safe and group-rule compliant**

---

## 1) Risk Tiers

### Tier 1 — Low Risk
- General encouragement
- Non-controversial tactical tips
- No links or soft non-promotional references

Action: Post with standard logging.

### Tier 2 — Medium Risk
- Branded links (OGS/2Nspira/book)
- Advice that implies outcomes
- Threads with mild disagreement dynamics

Action: Require review pass before posting.

### Tier 3 — High Risk
- Sensitive topics (legal, medical, politics, minors safety specifics)
- Heated conflict threads
- Claims that could be interpreted as guarantees
- Any situation likely to trigger admin scrutiny

Action: Do not post until manual sign-off.

---

## 2) Hard “Do Not” Rules
- Do not impersonate credentials Jeffrey doesn’t hold.
- Do not fabricate testimonials, stats, or outcomes.
- Do not flood groups with repetitive comments.
- Do not drop affiliate links without clear relevance.
- Do not hide affiliate relationship.
- Do not argue defensively with group members/admins.

---

## 3) Affiliate Link Policy (Amazon)
Affiliate links allowed only when all criteria are met:
1. The product solves the specific problem raised by poster.
2. The recommendation is defensible and non-exaggerated.
3. Add concise disclosure (example: “(affiliate link)”).
4. Prefer one product recommendation, not a list dump.
5. Do not post affiliate links where group rules prohibit them.

If unclear, post value-only response first and omit link.

---

## 4) Brand Voice Protection
Every response should sound like Jeffrey:
- Practical
- Respectful
- Experienced but not preachy
- Mission-driven (youth impact, leadership, responsible tech)

Avoid:
- Hype language
- Aggressive sales framing
- Condescending corrections

---

## 5) Incident Response
If negative feedback, admin warning, or backlash occurs:
1. Pause automated posting in affected group
2. Capture screenshots/context
3. Tag incident severity (S1/S2/S3)
4. Draft corrective response options
5. Escalate to Jeffrey for final direction

Post-incident:
- Root cause note
- Control update in SOP
- Team briefing
